---

_What was made to bind has come undone._

Soreth was once called the Crown, an enforcer of order among [[Sarim]], chosen again and again to bear Entropy because their strength never wavered. They believed endurance proved worth. That holding the burden longer made them greater. When others broke, Soreth stood strong.

They bore the burden far past what was safe. Not out of duty, but pride. And when the burden became too much, they did not fall. They rose, warped, convinced, burning with purpose.

They returned home as something new. Not broken. Not lost. Transformed. They killed [[Nepheth]], the keeper of return. They shattered [[Nepheth|Nepheth's]] [[Anchors|Anchor]] and destroyed their Scales so no call would bring them back. Then they left. No redemption. No final word.

Soreth has not stopped moving since.

They gather legions. They preach strength as salvation and call corruption a trial to be mastered. To mortals, they may seem radiant. To [[Gods]], persuasive. To their siblings, unrecognizable.

And yet they were one of the Thirteen Sarim. They were trusted. Cared for.

Still wearing the Crown. Still believing they are right.

Their [[Anchors|Anchor]] remains.

_If they fall, they may return. If the [[Anchors|Anchor]] breaks, they are gone forever. [[Sarim|The Sarim]] wait, blade half-drawn._

## The Fall - Complete Timeline

### Their Enduring Test (Years 2,080-2,155, 75 years)

Unlike the other Sarim, Soreth held Entropy for longer, enough to experience it directly. They learned its signature, its feeling, its nature. And in learning this, they developed a sensitivity no other Sarim possessed: they could separate Entropy's unending, overwriting madness from its magical potential.

It allowed them a new sight - to distinguish Entropy from natural creation through [[Path of Creation]].

**Key Development:**

- Learned to feel the difference between Entropy corruption and divine creation
- Believed this made them more effective hunters
- Saw themselves as mastering the burden others merely endured
- Pride disguised as dedication

### The Revelation (Years 2,135-2,155, ~20 years)

Soreth began to notice a pattern. Every god they hunted was using [[Path of Creation]]. Not spreading Entropy. Not corrupting mortals. Just creating. Making new races, new things, exercising divine prerogative.

Soreth tested their senses repeatedly. "This new race doesn't carry the Entropy signature. It's just... growth. Just creation."

**The truth crystallized, clear and terrible:**

- The Sarim were not hunting corruption
- They were hunting creation itself
- Gods were being destroyed for exercising their fundamental nature
- Growth was being stifled under the guise of preservation

### The Rejection (Years 2,155-2,165, ~10 years)

Soreth brought their findings to [[The Sovereign Eternal|Ezareth]]. Presented evidence. Demonstrated the distinction between Entropy and natural creation.

**Ezareth's response:** "You have held corruption too long. Your judgment is compromised. Return to rotation."

**The ultimate wound:**

- The one Sarim who could tell the difference was the one whose testimony couldn't be trusted
- Soreth's unique qualification was their disqualification
- Fear: "When I die and reforge, this truth dies with me. The system will continue unchanged."
- No one else could distinguish creation from corruption
- If Soreth let go now, nothing would change

### The Corruption (Years 2,165-2,200, 35 years)

Soreth refused to release the burden. They would not forget. They would not let the truth be lost.

But Entropy does not wait for noble intentions. The Stage 3 corruption they carried for so long began to twist everything. The truth they had seen clearly became justification for increasingly corrupted methods.

**Logic shift by Year 2,180:**

- The system is broken (true insight)
- Therefore, I must break the system (corrupted conclusion)
- Nepheth's cycle perpetuates the dogma (true observation)
- Therefore, I must kill Nepheth (corrupted solution)

**Final acceleration (Years 2,197-2,200):**

- Ezareth left for three years (legitimate emergency)
- Soreth bore triple burden alone
- Final push into absolute certainty
- Point of no return crossed

### The Murder (Year 2,200)

Soreth killed [[Nepheth]]. Shattered their sibling's [[Anchors|Anchor]]. Destroyed the Scales—ground them to powder and scattered the pieces to places Sarim could not easily reach.

**Soreth's belief:** They were liberating the system  
**Reality:** They had broken it permanently

They fled. They gathered legions. And even now, eight hundred and eighteen years later, leading corrupted forces across the planes, Soreth believes they are righteous.

They are the only one who saw the truth.  
They are also the only one who is irredeemably lost.

## Current State - Full Details

### Physical Appearance

Soreth still wears the Crown.

This is perhaps the most tragic element of their corruption. While other Stage 3 Entropic become visibly twisted, Soreth remains _recognizably themselves_. The Crown still sits geometric and gold (perhaps slightly darkened) upon their head. They retain the same number of arms, the same eye pattern, the same general silhouette.

The corruption lives in their eyes, their words, their absolute certainty—not in their form.

**Visual Markers:**

- **Crown:** Geometric gold, slightly darker/tarnished than before
- **Eyes:** Seven golden eyes in perfect circle, but the focus is _wrong_
- **Arms:** Six arms, same as always, but movements more precise, rigid
- **Wings:** Two black wings, perhaps with more geometric order to feathers
- **Tentacles:** Black with golden script, but patterns more angular
- **Armor:** Bronze and obsidian, unchanged from before

**To other Sarim:**

- Corruption obvious in how Soreth _moves_ and _speaks_
- Terrible certainty, rigid precision
- Inability to see themselves clearly

**To Soreth:**

- Looks exactly as they always did
- Proof they haven't fallen
- Proof they've simply _understood_ what others refuse to see

### The Crown's Second Function

Soreth has discovered—or believes they've discovered—the Crown's "true purpose."

The Crown can command living beings (its original function). But Soreth has learned it can also _perfect_ corrupted beings. By channeling their Stage 3 Entropy through the Crown's authority structure, they can transform screaming Scar-spawn into silent, obedient formations of geometric perfection.

They call this "absolution."

**The Process:**

1. Soreth approaches Entropic (Stage 1-2 Scar-spawn, corrupted entities)
2. The Crown pulses with cold, precise light
3. Authority flows from Crown into the corrupted form
4. Chaos solidifies into geometric perfection
5. What remains is silent, obedient, "purified"

**The Result: The Absolved**

- Entropic entities transformed into rigid, crystalline formations
- Move in perfect synchronization
- No screaming, no chaos, only silent purpose
- Arranged in geometric patterns
- Utterly obedient to Soreth's will
- The Absolved do not just look like crystals; they look like crude, forced imitations of  the Sarim. Soreth is trying to build a family out of monsters.

**Soreth's belief:**

> "This is what the Crown was always meant to do. Not command through voice, but through _correction_. I have not fallen. I have finally understood."

## The Anchor Dilemma

### The Situation

Soreth's [[Anchors|Anchor]] remains intact in the Sarim home chamber, guarded by [[Ezareth]].

**If Soreth dies and the Crown is recovered:**

- Could reforge in approximately **250 years** (current degraded system: 8 years × 30 due to massive Stage 3 Entropy corruption)
- **Without the Crown:** Would take ~2,500 years minimum (10x multiplier)

### The Philosophical Split

**Redemptionists** (preserve Anchor):

- Soreth was corrupted, not inherently evil
- They held the burden too long _for us_
- They saw truth we refused to acknowledge
- 250 years to potentially return clean
- Destroying Anchor = giving up on them forever
- What if they remember the truth when they return?

**Eliminationists** (destroy Anchor):

- Every day Soreth runs free, more damage done
- Stage 3 corruption may be permanent (reforging unproven at this level)
- Destroyed Nepheth's chance at return—showed no mercy
- Cannot risk them returning with memories intact
- Some sins are unforgivable
- System can't survive waiting 250 years

### Ezareth's Position

[[Ezareth]] has guarded the Anchor for 818 years. Has not destroyed it. Has not spoken publicly about which position they hold.

**Internal conflict:**

- Must choose between permanent loss and long-shot return
- Every passing day brings more damage
- Guilt: "I should have stayed. I should have seen."
- Hope: "They were corrupted, not evil. 250 years... maybe..."
- Pragmatism: "How many more will die while we wait?"

**Quote:**

> "You were my strongest. That's why you fell. That's why I failed you. That's why I stand here still, waiting to see if I was right to hope."

## For Players

### Encounter Framework

**First Encounter (Possible Deception):**

- May appear as helpful Sarim, aligned with players
- Offers assistance against common threat
- Subtle wrongness in behavior, "off" feeling
- Rhetoric about strength, endurance, broken systems
- Insight DC 25+ to detect (20+ if familiar with Sarim lore)

**Second Encounter (Growing Suspicion):**

- Inconsistencies emerge in knowledge or methods
- NPCs or other Sarim express concern
- Players may witness The Absolved in action
- Pattern of "perfecting" rather than destroying corruption
- Geometric precision feels wrong for preservation-focused being

**Final Confrontation:**

- Still believes they are righteous
- May offer players philosophical choice: "Join me in breaking a broken system"
- Commands The Absolved against party
- Will retreat if fight turns badly (patient, strategic, has long game)
- May leave prophetic warning: "You'll see. When you bear it long enough, you'll understand."

### Player Choices

**If Players Discover the Anchor:**

- **Destroy it:** Permanent death, eliminationist position, pragmatic
- **Guard it:** Preserve chance of return, redemptionist position, hopeful
- **Conceal it:** Delay the choice, kick can down road, political

**If Players Capture the Crown:**

- **Return to Ezareth:** Enable potential reforging (250 years)
- **Destroy it:** Extend reforging to 2,500+ years (near-permanent)
- **Keep it:** Wield legendary power, slowly die/transform
- **Hide it:** Prevent reforging entirely, deny Sarim the choice

**If Players Hear His Rhetoric:**

- He's not entirely wrong about the system being rigid
- His methods are absolutely monstrous
- Creates philosophical question: reform vs. rebellion
- No easy answers—players must decide what they believe

**Crown as Item (if recovered):**

- Provides immense power (CR boost, command abilities, create Absolved)
- Slowly transforms/corrupts wielder (geometric thinking, rigid certainty)
- Enable Soreth's eventual reforging if returned to Anchor
- Impossible for most mortals to wield without dying

## The Tragedy (DM Thematic Notes)

**What makes Soreth tragic:**

**Soreth was right:**

- The Sarim _were_ hunting creation, not just corruption
- The system _was_ rigid
- Growth _was_ being stifled under preservation
- They had genuine insight no other Sarim possessed

**Soreth is corrupted:**

- Methods are monstrous (murder, destruction, enslavement)
- Certainty is absolute delusion
- "Absolution" is corruption wearing mask of salvation
- Cannot see how far they've fallen

**The deeper questions:**

1. Was it the corruption that made Soreth break the system?
2. Or was it the system that made Soreth keep corruption until it broke them?
3. If they return in 250 years, reforged clean, will they remember the truth?
4. If they remember, will they fall again? Was the fall inevitable?
5. What defines failure: breaking under burden, or refusing to break?

**The irony:**

- Soreth is proof of Sarim dogma: "See? Even the one who tried to understand growth became corrupted. This is why we preserve, not adapt."
- But deeper irony: It was Sarim rigidity and rejection that contributed to the fall
- Had they listened when Soreth was still clear, perhaps things would have been different
- Both sides can claim vindication; neither can claim innocence

**For your table:**

- Soreth's story asks: Can a rigid system adapt without breaking?
- No easy answers—players must grapple with complexity
- Some sins may be unforgivable; some systems may be irredeemable
- Sometimes being right doesn't prevent being wrong
- Corruption can wear the face of righteousness
