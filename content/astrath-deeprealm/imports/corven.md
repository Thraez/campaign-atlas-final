# Corven
*A smuggler-king of the [[02_Regions/Tidemarrow]] underworld, whose face is on a hundred wanted posters and a hundred liars.*

A legendary [[Underground]] figure whose name is woven into half the great heists of the past decades. He has not been seen in years, but his name still opens doors. Crews invoke him to recruit, to threaten, to close a deal. So many people have worn his face that the legend is no longer entirely under his control, if it ever was.

![[Corven.png]]

## Appearance

The stories agree on the shape of him: tall and wiry, late thirties to early forties, ash-blond hair parted right with a small cowlick at the crown. Clear blue eyes. Sharp cheekbones, defined jaw, thin nose, gentled by a faint mouth and a relaxed brow. A nick through the left eyebrow. He is most often described in a river-grey long coat with hidden pockets, the hem hanging unnaturally straight, and a signet ring set with a chipped dark stone. Faint scent of fennel and cedar.

Wanted posters and tavern stories have repeated these details so often that nobody is certain which were ever real. Most who claim to have met him also describe a calm voice, a slight smile, and the kind of stillness in a room that reads as confidence rather than caution.

## Rumors and Legends

Some say he never failed a heist that he himself led. Others say half the heists credited to him were never his work, only his name flown like a flag.

He has not been seen in years. Younger crews have begun insisting he is dead. Older ones laugh at younger crews. There are smugglers who claim he hand-picks proxies and hires them out, that the men robbing vaults under his name are paid impersonators and he is the silent partner. There are smugglers who claim he is plotting something none of his proxies could match, in some cellar in [[02_Regions/Tidemarrow]] no one has the eye for. There are smugglers who refuse to speak of him at all, which is a different kind of answer.

Nobody agrees on which is the truth, and the doubt itself is now part of the legend.

## DM Notes

### Truth (unresolved)

Three live possibilities, none committed:

1. **Alive, lying low.** He let the legend run while he stepped back. Plotting something larger, more patient, harder to trace.
2. **Alive, running proxies.** Several impersonators are sanctioned, paid, fed instructions. The real Corven directs from somewhere safer.
3. **Dead.** The legend kept walking on its own momentum. Imposters multiplied without anyone left to disagree.

The party will encounter convincing fakes long before they encounter anything resembling truth. Pick a possibility when the campaign forces a commitment, not before.

### At-table cues

- **Voice:** calm, low, unhurried. He never raises it because he never needs to.
- **Mannerism:** charismatic, bold, overconfident, scheming. The composure is a performance of confidence, not its absence.
- **Tells:** two-finger tap on the table before a deal. Pause before naming a number. Looks at hands first, then eyes.
- **Body:** relaxed posture, slight smile.

### Real-vs-fake tells

When the party needs to test whether the Corven in front of them is the man, a sanctioned proxy, or a chancer wearing the face:

- **Onyx signet:** real piece is dark stone with a tiny star nick on the left edge. Most fakes use painted brass.
- **Coat hem:** real Corven's coat (or his Engineering robe variant) has hand-sewn hem weights used by field surveyors, not classroom cuts.
- **Coat condition:** real Corven keeps it clean. Fakes let the cloth take ore dust that refracts near a lamp.
- **Gait:** the real man's right foot angles a touch outward. Many imposters over-stride trying to look confident.
- **Scent:** fennel and cedar, faint. Most fakes don't bother.

### Mask recipe (used by Shifters and proxies)

Ash-blond wig parted right. Leather half-mask to sharpen the jaw. Blue glass bead pinned inside the collar to brighten eye color under lamplight. Weighted coat hem. Brass signet copy. The surface manner (calm voice, two-finger tap, pause before numbers) practiced as table behavior.

### Plot hooks

- Any encounter with "Corven" is a question, not an answer. Who is this person, who hired them, what do they actually know about the real man.
- If alive, the real Corven reacts to public uses of his name. A crew that abuses the legend may attract a quiet correction.
- Whoever runs the proxies (if option 2) is the actual power behind the legend. That figure is not yet decided.
